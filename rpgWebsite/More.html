Did You Know?

In the world of RPGs, your character's fate can hinge on the roll of a 20-sided die, but did you know that the iconic d20 design was not always standard? In the early days, Gary Gygax, co-creator of Dungeons and Dragons, used a 12-sided die and glued numbers on it for his first role-playing game. The shift to the now-famous 20 sides happened later, forever altering the destiny of countless imaginary heroes and villains.

RPG Realism: Inventory Edition

Ever wonder how RPG characters carry so much stuff? In the real world, it might be a struggle to manage a backpack, but not for our intrepid adventurers. The average RPG character can haul around tons of gear without breaking a sweat. To put it in perspective, the weight of their inventory would make even the most seasoned backpacker jealous. Forget about packing light; in the RPG realm, characters are the ultimate overburdened travelers.
